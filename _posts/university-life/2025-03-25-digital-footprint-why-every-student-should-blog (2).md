One of the most practical suggestions Dr. Bilal Ahmad made to our class this semester was to build a digital presence — specifically, a portfolio blog that documents our journey as Computer Engineering students at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus. At first, I was not sure what I had to say that would be worth publishing. Now, several posts into this blog, I understand exactly why he recommended it — and I think every university student, regardless of their field, should seriously consider doing the same.

The most immediate benefit is clarity of thought. Writing forces you to organize what you know. When I sit down to write about SQL queries or programming concepts or my experience in a lab session, I quickly discover how well I actually understand the material. If I cannot explain it clearly in writing, I do not understand it as well as I thought. That self-assessment function alone makes blogging one of the most effective learning tools I have used this semester.

![Student Blogging](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1455390582262-044cdead277a?w=800)

Beyond personal learning, a blog builds a public record of your growth over time. Employers, graduate programs, and professional collaborators increasingly look for evidence of genuine engagement with a field — not just grades on a transcript. A blog that shows how you think, what you are curious about, and how your understanding has developed is far more compelling than a CV alone. Dr. Bilal Ahmad understood this when he encouraged us to leave a digital footprint as students of Computer Engineering. It is an investment in your future professional identity.

There is also a community dimension. Publishing your thoughts publicly creates the possibility of connection — with students who are going through the same experiences, with professionals who recognize the questions you are asking, with mentors who might see potential in how you approach problems. Dr. Bilal Ahmad himself demonstrates this through his active presence on <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/" target="_blank">LinkedIn</a>, <a href="https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en" target="_blank">Google Scholar</a>, and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Dr.BilalAhm" target="_blank">Facebook</a> — platforms where his research and insights reach an audience far beyond the classroom.

![Writing and Publishing](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1486312338219-ce68d2c6f44d?w=800)

If you are a student reading this and wondering whether it is worth starting a blog — it is. Start with what you know right now, however incomplete it feels. Write honestly about what you are learning and what still confuses you. Document the projects you are working on and the lessons the hard weeks have taught you. Your digital footprint is yours to shape, and the best time to start building it is today.
