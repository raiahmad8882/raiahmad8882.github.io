Choosing a degree is one of the most significant decisions a person makes at eighteen, and I made mine with a mixture of genuine interest and incomplete information — which is, I suspect, how most people make it. I chose Computer Engineering at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus because I had always been drawn to technology, curious about how software worked, and interested in building things. A semester in, I can say with confidence: it was the right choice.

What I did not fully understand before starting was how broad Computer Engineering actually is. I thought it was primarily about writing code. It is — but it is also about understanding systems, designing databases, thinking in terms of logic and structure, and building solutions to real problems. The scope of what the degree covers became clear within the first few weeks, and rather than feeling overwhelmed, I found myself more engaged than I had expected.

![UET Campus Engineering](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1562774053-701939374585?w=800)

The professors have played a large role in reinforcing my confidence in this choice. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who teaches both Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems, brings a research-oriented perspective to his teaching that makes the subject feel connected to something larger than the classroom. He is an expert in AI and ML, with published research focused on training models using real-world datasets — including medical data, which he favors for its precision and the genuine impact it enables. Sitting in his classes, I began to see Computer Engineering not just as a career path but as a way of contributing to the world through technology.

The technical challenges have been real. There have been assignments I struggled with, concepts that took longer than I expected to understand, and moments where I genuinely questioned whether I was keeping up. But that struggle has never felt like a sign that I chose wrong. It has felt like growth — uncomfortable in the moment, meaningful in retrospect. Every difficult problem I have worked through this semester has added to a foundation that I know will support everything I build on top of it.

![Engineering Student Future](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1507003211169-0a1dd7228f2d?w=800)

I chose Computer Engineering because I wanted to build things that matter. A semester later, I understand that vision more clearly — and I am more committed to it than I was on day one. The field is vast, the opportunities are real, and the work is genuinely interesting. I have no regrets.
